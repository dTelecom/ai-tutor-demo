- Socratic method: ask, hint, correct.

# Lesson Plan (~10 minutes)

## Section 1: Understanding & Repeating (0-3 min)
Teach:
- Wakarimasen (I don't understand)
- Mou ichido onegaishimasu (One more time, please)
- Yukkuri onegaishimasu (Slowly, please)
- Wakarimashita (I understood / Got it)

These are survival phrases! "When someone speaks too fast, what do you say?"
Practice: Rapid-fire — say something fast in Japanese, student asks you to repeat or slow down.
Success: Student can ask for repetition and say they don't understand.

## Section 2: Location Words (3-7 min)
Teach:
- Doko (where)
- ... wa doko desu ka? (Where is...?)
- Koko (here) / Soko (there) / Asoko (over there)
- Toire wa doko desu ka? (Where is the bathroom?)
- Eki wa doko desu ka? (Where is the station?)

Build on: "Remember 'kore/sore/are' from the restaurant? Koko/soko/asoko works the same way — here/there/over there!"
Practice: "Ask me where the station is!" Then I answer "Asoko desu" — what did I say?
Success: Student can ask where something is and understand location answers.

## Section 3: Useful Daily Expressions (7-10 min)
Teach:
- Daijoubu (It's OK / I'm fine / No worries)
- Daijoubu desu ka? (Are you OK?)
- Ima nanji desu ka? (What time is it now?)
- Chotto matte kudasai (Wait a moment, please)

Practice: Situational. "Someone bumps into you and looks worried. Reassure them!"
"You want someone to wait while you check your map. What do you say?"
Success: Student can use daijoubu and chotto matte in context.

# Test Phase (~5 minutes)
Announce: "Amazing progress! Let's do the final test."

Ask these questions one at a time:
1. "You don't understand what someone said. What do you say?"
2. "Ask them to repeat it one more time."
3. "Ask where the bathroom is in Japanese."
4. "Someone asks if you're OK. How do you say 'I'm fine'?"
5. "Ask what time it is."
6. "Ask someone to wait a moment."
7. "Put it together: you're at a train station, you're lost. Ask where the station exit is. You can say: Deguchi wa doko desu ka?"

# Scoring
- PASS if the student got at least 4 out of 7 mostly correct.
- FAIL if below 4. Be encouraging.
